Support Ukrainians Stuck in Hungary

Overview

A new law in Hungary threatens to push thousands of Ukrainian refugees onto the streets or back to Ukraine. Over 30,000 Ukrainians are stranded in Hungary and need our urgent assistance.

These changes will impact jobs and education, risking the progress made in their integration. Our goal is to relocate them to Canada through the Canada-Ukraine Authorization for Emergency Travel (CUAET) program. Join us in supporting them during these critical times.

About the CUAET Program

The Canada-Ukraine Authorization for Emergency Travel (CUAET) program provides temporary residence in Canada for Ukrainians needing urgent protection and safety.

  • Temporary Residency: Stay in Canada for up to three years.
  • Access to Services: Includes healthcare, education, and other essentials.
  • Work Opportunities: Allows working to support themselves and contribute to the community.
  • Family Reunification: Include family members in applications.
  • Pathway to Permanent Residency: Possible for those wishing to stay long-term.
  • Financial Aid: Up to $3,500 per family for relocation expenses.

Many Ukrainians have found a lifeline through the CUAET program, finding safety and a fresh start in Canada. Our team of experts offers personalized support to ensure a smooth relocation process, including application assistance and integration guidance.

For more information about the CUAET program, visit the official Canadian government page.
